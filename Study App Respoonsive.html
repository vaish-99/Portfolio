Determining Students Current Study Patterns

Primary Research


Desk research provided preliminary quantitative insights into students' beliefs about studying. To build on these findings and gain a deeper understanding of self-study patterns, I chose a quantitative approach through a survey consisting of 15 multiple-choice questions. 12 responses were obtained and medium of conducting the survey was Google Forms.

Observations

Students Need to Adapt Productive Study Strategies

Research Insights


1.Effective study habits are often underutilized, as students tend to choose passive strategies more frequently.

2.Many students hold misconceptions about learning techniques, which demonstrates a disconnect between their understanding of effective strategies and their actual implementation.

3.Analysis of the results and statistics reveals three key areas of study engagement: Consistency, Active learning, and Self-Evaluation.

Curriculum design Dictates Current Behaviors

Context Analysis

As the curriculum progresses, students get busy with assignments and other commitments, which impacts their study motivation. Study motivation tends to dip twice around exams, creating ideal moments to send study session reminders. The curriculum can be divided into four main stages: Academic Onboarding, Study Planning, Focused Learning, and Evaluation
